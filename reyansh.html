Fun Facts about the Planets!

Our solar system consists of eights planets: Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune.

By:

Reyansh Sawant

March 2, 2024

Together, the Sun and the planets produced the solar nebula, a cloud of gas and dust, some 4.6 billion years ago. The collapse of the solar nebula was most likely caused by a shock wave from a nearby supernova explosion. The planets formed in a narrow disk circling the Sun, which formed at its core.

Earth formed when gravity pulled swirling gas and dust in to become the third planet from the Sun.
