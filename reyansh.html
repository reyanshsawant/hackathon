Fun Facts about the Planets!

Our solar system consists of eight planets: Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune. RIP Pluto.

By:

Reyansh Sawant

March 2, 2024

Together, the Sun and the planets produced the solar nebula, a cloud of gas and dust, some 4.6 billion years ago. The collapse of the solar nebula was most likely caused by a shock wave from a nearby supernova explosion. The planets formed in a narrow disk circling the Sun, which formed at its core.

Earth formed when gravity pulled swirling gas and dust in to become the third planet from the Sun.

In Order (by Distance from Sun)

  • Mercury

    Mercury is the smallest planet in our solar system and the closest to the Sun; it is little bigger than the moon. The Sun would appear more than three times as large and the sunlight up to seven times brighter from Mercury's surface than it does from Earth.

  • Venus

    On Venus, a day lasts more than a year. Venus takes longer to complete one rotation on its axis than it does to complete one orbit around the Sun. To revolve once, that would take 243 Earth days.

  • Earth

    While Earth is only the fifth largest planet in the solar system, it is the only world in our solar system with liquid water on the surface.

  • Mars

    Mars is a rocky planet. Its solid surface has been altered by volcanoes, impacts, winds, crustal movement and chemical reactions.

  • Jupiter

    Jupiter has the shortest day in the solar system. One day on Jupiter takes only about 10 hours.

  • Saturn

    Saturn is one of five planets able to be seen with the naked eye. Saturn has the second-shortest day in the solar system. One day on Saturn takes only 10.7 hours.

    • Uranus

    The planet Uranus is extremely windy and frigid. It revolves at a about 90-degree angle, encircled by faint rings and around twenty tiny moons.

    Neptune

    Neptune was the first planet located using math. A day on Neptune is 16 hours. A year on Neptune is about 165 Earth years.
